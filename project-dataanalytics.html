Project Overview

As part of an AI & Data Analytics group project, I built a Market Attractiveness Index in Power BI to evaluate and rank 13 countries for strategic market entry. By integrating global datasets and engineering advanced DAX measures, the analysis surfaced a clear, data-backed sequence for international expansion — culminating in a two-phase strategic roadmap that balanced near-term revenue with long-run growth ambition.

Key Contributions

Power BI Dashboarding

Developed an interactive Market Attractiveness Index in Power BI, integrating global datasets to rank 13 countries by weighted revenue potential and acquisition cost — giving stakeholders a live, filterable view of market opportunity.

DAX Engineering & RANKX Analysis

Engineered DAX measures using RANKX to run multi-variable trade-off analysis across revenue, cost, and competitive intensity — identifying the optimal market entry sequence with quantified justification for each ranking decision.

Two-Phase Market Roadmap

Authored a strategic two-phase roadmap: Phase 1 targets established markets for near-term revenue generation; Phase 2 sequences entry into high-competition, high-cost regions once brand and operational footing is secured.

Global Dataset Integration

Sourced and unified heterogeneous global datasets — normalising metrics across different economic scales and currencies to ensure the index weights were consistent, comparable, and analytically sound.

Reflection & Learning

  • Building the Market Attractiveness Index taught me how to turn ambiguous business questions into structured, quantitative frameworks — defining the right variables and weights was as analytical as the DAX code itself.
  • Engineering RANKX measures across multiple dimensions deepened my understanding of multi-criteria decision analysis and how small weighting changes can flip a country's ranking significantly.
  • Integrating global datasets surfaced real challenges around data normalisation — I learned to account for scale differences, missing values, and conflicting source methodologies before any modelling could begin.
  • Authoring the two-phase roadmap pushed me to think beyond the dashboard and translate analytical output into executive-ready strategy, balancing financial logic with real-world risk appetite.
  • With more time, I would build a scenario simulation layer in Power BI so stakeholders could adjust weighting assumptions interactively and see how the country rankings respond in real time.
